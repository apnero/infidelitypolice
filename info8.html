Connecticut Private Investigator Offers Advice on How to Handle Opposite Sex Relationships

Leading private investigator says that just because a spouse has a close relationship with a member of the opposite sex, it does not mean that the spouse is being unfaithful.

“It can certainly be problematic for a marriage if one person engages in what may be perceived as threatening behavior, such as having a close friend that is a member of the opposite sex,” says Christopher Paoletti, Founder and President of Infidelity Investigations (http://www.infidelitypolice.com), a Connecticut private investigation firm that specializes in infidelity cases. “When ever a friendship blossoms between a spouse and a member of the opposite sex, the married couple should have a frank discussion about the slippery slope of familiarity that is always the beginning of marital infidelity.”

Paoletti says that it is never easy to discuss a spouse’s friendship with a member of the opposite sex, particularly when they may be potentially building bonds. Spouses do not want to seem jealous or controlling, he says, but these sorts of relationships can be the precursor to an affair.

“Most people who end up cheating on their spouse rarely begin with ill intentions. They simply believe they are just friends,” says Paoletti. “However, when any married individual defends an opposite-sex relationship with the ‘we’re just friends’ excuse, a red flag should go up.”

Paoletti says that if a spouse is concerned about a competing opposite-sex relationship, that relationship should be suspended until the marriage can be stabilized and a safe connection is established again.

“To continue in that competing relationship, regardless of how threatening it might actually be, is harmful to the long-term trust and safety of the marriage,” says Paoletti. “I’ve seen so many individuals unassumingly slide into an affair from seemingly innocent interactions with the opposite sex. As a result, I encourage caution and boundaries in same-sex friendships.”

Paoletti says that, regardless of the arrangement, it needs to support the marriage. If the final friendship arrangement still makes a spouse uncomfortable, he or she should stay with the conversation until it can be resolved in the best interest of the marriage.

“The concerns of a spouse need to be heard and taken seriously,” says Paoletti. “An actual sexual affair doesn’t have to happen for trust to be damaged in a marriage. A relationship can suffer when partners don’t respond to each other’s fears and concerns.”
