Connecticut Private Investigator Says That Social Media Has Been Known to Ruin Relationships

Leading private investigator says that a cheating spouse doesn’t need social media to be unfaithful, but it can certainly fuel the fire.

According to Christopher Paoletti, Founder and President of Infidelity Investigations (http://www.infidelitypolice.com), a Connecticut private investigation firm that specializes in infidelity cases, text messaging and social networking websites, such as Facebook and MySpace, can not only bring people together, but they can also drive a wedge between couples as well.

“I hear repeatedly from clients that they are tired of playing second fiddle to digital devices,” says Paoletti. “Whether innocent or not, digital relationships not only place a strain on marriages, they arouse suspicion and, more often than not, that suspicion often turns out to be true.”

Paoletti says texting and participation in social networking does not break up a marriage, people do. However, opportunity is a key predictor of infidelity, and social media outlets have certainly increased opportunity.

"Decades ago, if a person found a co-worker interesting and later thought about calling them at home, just to say hello, they would have to ask themselves whether it was appropriate,” says Paoletti. “These sorts of stopgaps are now gone because texts and e-mails can be delivered privately. Sending a little message, at least at first, can be seemingly innocent.”

Now, Paoletti says, spouses can carry on an affair right under their partner’s nose.

Paoletti suggests that when it comes to social networking, spouses should share passwords to avoid fights and suspicion.

“The bottom line is, if you find yourself e-mailing something that you would not want your spouse to know about, you are in danger of crossing a line,” he says. “If you get a ‘friend’ request from an old flame, be open with your spouse about whether to reply or not.”

According to Paoletti, communication is the key to a healthy relationship.

“Anyone that sees an increase in text messaging or social media usage, particularly with a member of the opposite sex, should take those concerns very seriously,” says Paoletti. “Hiring a private investigator to confirm those suspicions is sometimes the best course of action.”
